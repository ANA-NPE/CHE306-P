How It Works

Five steps. One sweetening cycle.

Sour gas containing 45 mol% CO₂ and 1 mol% H₂S enters at 5,200 kPa. After two columns and a regeneration loop, it leaves at pipeline-spec quality.

01
Sour Gas Feed

A 500 kmol/h natural-gas stream enters the bottom of the absorber at 35 °C and 5,210 kPa. It carries 45 mol% CO₂ and 1 mol% H₂S — both must come out before the gas can enter the pipeline.

500 kmol/h · 5,210 kPa
02
Counter-Current Absorption

In T-101's 30-stage column, MDEA + Sulfolane solvent flows down while the gas rises. CO₂ reacts chemically with MDEA; Sulfolane provides physical absorption capacity at high partial pressure.

30 stages · ~15 m bed
03
Sweet Gas Out

Treated gas leaves the top of the absorber with CO₂ reduced from 45% to 2.94 mol% and H₂S to essentially zero — meeting pipeline specifications. Methane content rises from 52% to 93.7%.

CO₂ 2.94% · H₂S ≈ 0
04
Solvent Regeneration

Rich amine is depressurised through the flash drum, pre-heated by the lean/rich exchanger, and stripped of acid gases in T-103's 25-stage column at 500 kPa with reboiler heat input.

25 stages · 500 kPa
05
CO₂ Vent · Amine Recycle

~97 kmol/h of 60% CO₂ stream is vented from the stripper top. 1,893 kmol/h of regenerated lean amine returns to the absorber, completing the cycle. Mass balance closes to 0.06%.

CO₂ vent · Loop closed
06
Validated & Safe

Stage counts, balances, and compositions all match Aspen HYSYS V14 simulation. A layered protection strategy guards against H₂S toxicity, foaming, blow-by, and reboiler thermal excursion.

HYSYS-validated · HAZOP-ready
Process Flow

The full cycle.

Sour gas enters the absorber at the bottom; sweet gas leaves the top. Rich amine flows through a flash drum and lean/rich exchanger before regeneration in the reboiled stripper.

Key: Sienna = sour / acid-gas streams · Sage = sweet gas product · Goldenrod = amine streams. The lean/rich exchanger recovers heat to pre-heat the rich solvent before stripping.

Design Calculations

Mass. Energy. Stages.

Independent hand calculations of mass and energy balances, Kremser equilibrium-stage analysis, and HTU/NTU packed-bed sizing — all cross-checked against the rigorous simulation.

01
Mass balance closes to 0.06%

At steady state with 30 stages and zero accumulation, FSourGas + FLeanAmine = FSweetGas + FRichAmine. Hand calc and HYSYS agree exactly on a molar basis and to within 0.06% on mass.

500 + 1,893 = 226.4 + 2,166.6 = 2,393 kmol/h ✓
02
T-101 runs adiabatically

Total enthalpy in equals total enthalpy out. The exothermic CO₂ absorption heat is carried out by a 6.4 °C rise in the rich-amine stream — no external cooling is required.

Qabs = ΣHout − ΣHin ≈ 0 kJ/h
ΔTsolvent = 32.5 − 26.1 = 6.4 °C
03
Kremser absorber → 30 stages

Solute-free mole ratios with effective slope m = 6.945 (back-fitted to Aspen's rigorous VLE). Absorption factor A = 0.991 — just above the theoretical minimum solvent rate.

YN+1/Y1 = 0.8182 / 0.03029 = 27.0
A = L/(m·Vs) = 1,892/(6.945·275) = 0.991
⇒ N ≈ 30 ✓
04
Kremser stripper → 25 stages

Apparent slope m = 12.55 reflects the steep desorption curve. Stripping factor S = 1.244 confirms vapour as the favoured phase for CO₂.

S = m·V/L = (12.55·200)/2,018 = 1.244
xin/xout = 0.0599/0.00005 = 1,198
⇒ N ≈ 25 ✓
05
HTU / NTU → ~15 m bed

Sulzer Mellapak 250Y structured packing. Both columns converge to ~15 m of packed bed — a reasonable preliminary value for an MDEA / Sulfolane sweetening service.

Absorber: NTU 30.2 · HTU 0.50 m → H ≈ 15.0 m
Stripper: NTU 22.4 · HTU 0.67 m → H ≈ 15.0 m
06
Solvent system: hybrid by design

MDEA provides selective chemical absorption with low regeneration energy and low corrosion tendency. Sulfolane adds physical absorption capacity at the high CO₂ partial pressure of the feed. Together: high removal, manageable reboiler duty.

MDEA 33.9% · Sulfolane 34.0% · H₂O 21.2% · CO₂ 10.2%

Safety Evaluation

Sour service, layered defence.

High-pressure sour service introduces toxicity, asphyxiation, flammability, corrosion, and process-upset hazards. Each is paired with a preventive and protective layer — from inherent safety up to flare routing.

☠️ H₂S Toxic Release
Leak from absorber, flash drum, stripper overhead, sample point or rich-amine line
Safeguards: Fixed H₂S detectors (10 / 20 ppm alarms), portable detectors, permit-to-work, escape SCBA, closed sampling, vent routing to flare.
😶‍🌫️ CO₂ Asphyxiation
CO₂-rich vent or leak displacing oxygen in low-lying / enclosed areas
Safeguards: Stripper overhead routed to safe-elevation stack with adequate dispersion · oxygen monitoring in adjacent enclosed areas.
🔥 Methane Fire / Explosion
Sweet-gas leak mixing with air within the flammable range
Safeguards: Hydrocarbon detectors · hazardous-area electrical classification · ignition-source control · grounding · N₂ purge at startup.
🧱 Sulfide Stress Cracking / HIC
Wet H₂S hydrogen damage near welds — cf. Union Oil Romeoville absorber rupture (OSHA 1986)
Safeguards: Sour-service material selection · HIC-resistant plate · post-weld heat treatment · hardness control · weld inspection.
🧪 Amine Stress Corrosion Cracking
Hot lean amine + residual weld stress (Bagdasarian, Shargay & Coombs 1992)
Safeguards: Stress-relieved welds in lean-amine and stripper service · lean-amine temperature control · inspection of hot-amine circuits.
💧 Erosion-Corrosion at Letdown
Two-phase flashing flow downstream of rich-amine pressure-reducing valve
Safeguards: Stainless / hard-faced trim · velocity control · corrosion monitoring · scheduled inspection.
🫧 Foaming & Carryover
Hydrocarbon contamination, FeS solids, degradation products, heat-stable salts
Safeguards: Inlet separation · coalescing filtration · solvent analysis · high-DP alarms · controlled antifoam (root-cause fix).
⬆️ Gas Blow-By to Flash Drum
Loss of absorber bottom level exposing flash drum to absorber pressure
Safeguards: Low-low level interlock · PSV sized for blow-by · relief to closed flare per API 521.
🔥 Reboiler Overheating
Loss of rich-amine flow while steam continues to the reboiler
Safeguards: Fail-closed steam valve · low-low flow trip · low-low level trip · high-temperature trip.
🚨 Acid-Gas Breakthrough
Loss of lean-amine circulation while sour gas continues to enter
Safeguards: Low-low lean-amine flow interlock closes the sour-gas inlet ESD valve. SIL set by formal LOPA per IEC 61511.

Final Word

A unit that works.

Within the scope of this CHE 306 design project, the proposed MDEA / Sulfolane absorption unit is technically feasible. It achieves strong CO₂ and H₂S removal, the mass and energy balances are consistent, and Kremser shortcut and HYSYS rigorous results agree on stage count. With rate-based validation, economic optimisation, and detailed HAZOP / LOPA, the unit can be designed for safe and effective sour natural gas sweetening.
